## Quick Start Guide

### Your First 5 Minutes

**Step 1: Access the Dashboard**
- Navigate to: `https://fa-ai-assistant.com/dashboard`
- Log in with your credentials
- You'll see your portfolio stocks with their latest summaries

**Step 2: Review Your Portfolio**
- Each stock shows three summary types: **Hook**, **Medium**, and **Expanded**
- Green indicators = Fresh summaries (< 24 hours)
- Yellow indicators = Slightly stale (24-48 hours)
- Red indicators = Needs refresh (> 48 hours)

**Step 3: Try a Quick Summary**
- Click on any stock ticker (e.g., "AAPL")
- Read the **Hook Summary** first (25-50 words)
- If you need more detail, click "Show Medium" or "Show Expanded"

**Step 4: Ask a Question**
- Click the "Ask AI" button in the top right
- Type: "What happened with Apple this week?"
- Get an instant, cited answer in seconds

**That's it!** You're ready to use the system.

## Understanding the Three Summary Types

### Hook Summaries (25-50 words)

**Purpose:** Quick portfolio monitoring and daily briefings

**When to Use:**
- Daily portfolio review (scan 100+ stocks in 10 minutes)
- Email updates to clients
- Morning market briefings
- Quick checks before calls

**What You Get:**
- Single most important recent development
- Specific numbers and dates
- Active voice, no fluff
- Average reading time: 10-15 seconds

**Example:**
> **AAPL - Hook Summary (35 words)**
>
> Apple reported Q4 2024 revenue of $394.3B, up 8% YoY, driven by strong iPhone 15 Pro demand. Services revenue hit record $85.2B (+16%). Company announced $110B buyback and 4% dividend increase.
>
> *Sources: 10-K Filing (Nov 1, 2024), FactSet (Nov 2, 2024)*

**Best For:** Portfolio managers monitoring hundreds of stocks, morning briefings

---

**When to Use:**

- Quarterly portfolio reviews
- Team briefings and alignment

- Comprehensive financial metrics with context
- Segment performance breakdown

## Interactive Query System

### How to Ask Questions

**The AI understands natural language - just ask like you would a colleague.**

**Good Questions:**
- "What was Apple's revenue last quarter?"
- "How is Microsoft's cloud business performing?"
- "Compare Netflix and Disney+ subscriber growth"
- "What are the biggest risks for Tesla?"
- "Why did Amazon's stock drop today?"
- "What analysts are saying about Nvidia outlook?"

**Questions to Avoid:**
- Too vague: "Tell me about Apple" (Try: "What are Apple's latest earnings results?")
- Not finance-related: "What's the weather?" (System only knows financial data)
- Future predictions: "Will Apple stock go up?" (System provides data, not predictions)

---

### Understanding Query Types

The system automatically classifies your question and tailors the response:

**HOOK-Level Questions** (Quick facts)
- "What was revenue?"
- "What's the price target?"
- "When is the next earnings date?"

**Response:** Short, factual answer with citation

**MEDIUM-Level Questions** (Analysis)
- "How is the company performing?"
- "What happened in the earnings call?"
- "What's the outlook for next quarter?"

**Response:** 2-3 paragraph analysis with multiple sources

**DEEP-Level Questions** (Research)
- "Compare this company to competitors"
- "What are the long-term growth drivers?"
- "Analyze the risks and opportunities"

**Response:** Comprehensive research brief with 5+ citations

### Maximizing Accuracy

**The system achieves 95%+ accuracy through multi-source validation, but you should:**

1. **Check Citations for Critical Claims**
   - If you're sharing a number with a client, click the citation
   - Verify it matches the source document
   - Look for "Confidence: High" indicator

2. **Cross-Reference Multiple Sources**
   - Best summaries cite 3+ sources (EDGAR, BlueMatrix, FactSet)
   - If claim has only 1 source, consider additional research
   - Look for consensus across sources

3. **Watch for Uncertainty Language**
   - "Approximately" = estimate, not exact figure
   - "According to analyst reports" = sell-side view, not company guidance
   - "May" or "Could" = forward-looking, not confirmed

4. **Report Issues**
   - If you spot an error, click "Report Error" button
   - System learns from feedback to improve
   - You'll get confirmation when fixed

### How to Verify a Citation

**Step-by-Step:**

1. **Click the Citation Link**
   - Opens source document in new tab
   - Jumps to relevant section automatically

2. **Review the Quote**
   - Highlighted text shows exact source
   - Verify it matches the claim

3. **Check the Date**
   - Ensure it's current (< 90 days for most uses)
   - Older filings may be superseded

4. **Read Context**
   - Read paragraph before and after
   - Make sure claim isn't taken out of context

5. **Look for Updates**
   - Check if newer filing supersedes this one
   - Click "Show Latest" if available

**When to Verify:**
- All claims shared directly with clients
- Numbers used in investment memos
- Claims that surprise you
- Before making portfolio decisions

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Issue: Summary is missing for a stock**

**Solution:**
- Stock may not be in coverage universe (1,000 stocks)
- Click "Request Coverage" to add to nightly batch
- Alternatively, use Interactive Query: "Summarize [TICKER]"
- Manual summary generated in < 60 seconds

---

**Issue: Summary is stale (> 48 hours old)**

**Solution:**
- Nightly batch may have failed for this stock
- Click "Refresh Now" to generate new summary
- Takes 30-60 seconds
- If issue persists, report via "Report Issue" button

---

**Issue: Citation link is broken**

**Solution:**
- Source document may have moved
- Try clicking "View Alternative Source"
- If multiple citations, use another source
- Report broken link via "Report Issue" button

---

**Issue: AI answer seems wrong**

**Solution:**
- Click citations to verify source
- If source contradicts AI answer, click "Report Error"
- System flags for human review
- You'll get email when corrected
- Use alternative source in meantime

---

**Issue: Query response is too slow (> 60 seconds)**

**Solution:**
- Complex multi-company queries may take longer
- Check "Query Queue" to see processing status
- Break complex question into simpler parts
- Example: Instead of "Compare all FAANG stocks", ask about each individually
- If consistently slow, report performance issue

---

**Issue: Can't find a specific stock**

**Solution:**
- Try searching by full company name, not ticker
- Example: "Apple Inc" instead of "AAPL"
- Check spelling (GOOGL vs GOOG)
- If stock is not in coverage, click "Request Coverage"

4. Ask AI: "What are the top risks in this portfolio?"

**Time:** 45 min (vs 4 hours manually)

### Workflow 2: Earnings Season Monitoring

**During earnings season (4 weeks/quarter):**

1. Set up alerts for "Earnings Announcements"
2. Each morning, receive Hook summaries for all companies reporting
3. Scan for beats/misses (5 min)
4. For client holdings that reported:
   - Open Medium summary
   - Ask AI: "What drove the beat/miss?"
   - Call client if material
5. Track all earnings in spreadsheet (auto-exported)

**Time:** 30 min/day during earnings (vs 2 hours/day)

**Quarterly rebalancing for 50 model portfolios:**

4. Research sell candidates with Expanded summaries

6. Use Compare feature for head-to-head analysis
7. Make rebalancing decisions
8. Document rationale with citations

**Time:** 1 day (vs 1 week manually)
